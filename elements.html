Hardware Components Description

Arduino Mega 2560

The Arduino Mega 2560 is a microcontroller board based on the ATmega2560. It's the heart of our Smart Parking System, responsible for controlling various components and processing data.

IR Sensors

IR (Infrared) sensors are used to detect the presence of vehicles in parking slots. They emit and receive infrared signals, allowing us to determine whether a parking slot is occupied or vacant.

Servo Motor

A servo motor is used to control the movement of barriers or gates at the entrance and exit of the parking area. It ensures secure access control.

16x2 LCD with I2C

The 16x2 LCD display with I2C interface is used to provide real-time information to users. It displays parking slot occupancy status and other relevant information.

Jumper Wires

Jumper wires are essential for making electrical connections between various components of the Smart Parking System. They ensure reliable and efficient communication.

Features

The Smart Parking System primarily utilizes IR sensors to detect the presence of vehicles in parking slots. This information is then displayed on the 16x2 LCD with I2C, allowing users to quickly identify available parking spaces. The dashboard shows if the parking slots are occupied with the help of the IR sensors present in the respective slots. When a vehicle enters the parking station, the IR sensor at the gate detects its presence, and the barrier is lifted with the help of a servo motor if there are available slots in the station. Otherwise, the user is notified that all the spaces are in use through the dashboard.
